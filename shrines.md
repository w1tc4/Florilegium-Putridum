# shrines and monuments


| Word | Language | Literal Meaning | Connotation / Vibe |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Reliquary** | Latin | A container for holy relics | Macabre, holding bones or physical remains. |
| **Sacrarium** | Latin | A sanctuary or sacred drain | Archaic, occult, and hidden from view. |
| **Altar**     | Latin | A high place for sacrifice | Ritualistic, sacrificial, or blood-stained. |
| **Sanctum**   | Latin | A holy or private place | Secretive, forbidden, and highly exclusive. |
| **Sepulcher** | Latin / Greek | A stone tomb or burial cave | Grim, death-centric, housing a corpse. |
| **Mausoleum** | Greek | A magnificent, stately tomb | Imposing, cold, monumental reverence for the dead. |
| **Cenotaph** | Greek | An empty tomb | Ghostly, tragic, honoring someone lost elsewhere. |
| **Charnel House** | Middle English | A house for skeletal remains | Overwhelmingly macabre, piled with mass bones. |
