# Tombs
| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Catacomb** | An underground cemetery or labyrinth of tunnels, often associated with death and decay. |
| **Ruin** | The physical collapse or total decay of a once-great building or civilization. |
| **Charnel** | A building or vault where corpses or bones are piled up. |
| **Ossuary** | A chest, building, or well meant to contain the bones of the dead. |
| **Crypt** | An underground stone chamber beneath a church, used for burials or secret meetings. |
| **Sepulchre** | A stone tomb, vault, or cave recess built specifically for interring a corpse. |
| **Sacrarium** | A sacred storage place, sometimes used to deposit holy or ruined relics. |
| **Barrow** | An ancient, earth-covered burial mound often containing hidden stone chambers. |
| **Cenotaph** | A monument or empty tomb built to honor people whose remains are elsewhere. |
| **Golgotha** | A place of execution, intense suffering, or a site covered in human skulls. |
| **Cairn** | A rough heap of stones serving as a prehistoric grave marker or monument. |
